# Adversarial Translation Protocol (v2.0)

## Objective
To produce a "Main Translation" that is not only readable but represents the most accurate and technically precise forensic reflection of the Hebrew source in the English language.

## 1. Lexical Intensity Standards
- **Verbs of Creation**: Never use "made" or "did." Use *specifically fashioned*, *architecturally constructed*, *strategically organized*, or *systemically implemented*.
- **Verbs of Perception**: Never use "saw." Use *forensically audited*, *visually inspected for integrity*, or *cognitively perceived*.
- **Verbs of Speech**: Never use "said." Use *legally commanded*, *sovereignly decreed*, or *forensically declared*.

## 2. Jurisdictional Accuracy
- Use legal terminology where the Hebrew implies naming, separation, or commandment (e.g., *jurisdiction*, *domain*, *decree*, *statute*, *legal title*).

## 3. Elaborative Precision
- Any word added for "smoothening" must be bracketed by asterisks and must be the **most accurate technical descriptor** of the underlying Hebrew morphology.
- **Example**: *bereshit* -> *absolute and uncaused beginning* (not just "the beginning").

## 4. Adversarial Conflict Resolution
- **Rule 80: Lexical Supremacy**: Every word in the `main-translation` must be critiqued by the adversarial agent. If multiple English words fit, the agent must not settle for the first viable option; they must audit all synonyms and select the absolute 'lexical superior'—the word with the highest forensic and ontological precision for that specific context.
- **Rule 81: Syntactic Flow & Clarity**: Forensic precision must not result in convoluted syntax. Every `main-translation` must be audited for narrative flow. Avoid excessive clausal nesting or redundant elaboration that obscures the primary action. The result must be a clean, high-intensity sentence that commands the reader's attention through clarity, not just complexity.
- If two English words compete for accuracy, the one with the higher **forensic intensity** and **etymological alignment** wins.
